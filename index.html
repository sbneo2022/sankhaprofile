About me

Welcome to my website. I am Sankha, serving as the Head of Product Strategy at BabylonChain.io. My expertise lies in using economic modeling to forge strategic partnerships, and crafting go-to-market strategies for Babylon's suite of products. As a protocol architect, I am devoted to solving challenging problems and building products within the Cosmos and Arbitrum ecosystems.

Transformative Blockchain Projects

I played an initial role as a protocol designer/implementer for a groundbreaking project Thorchain. I designed the arbitrage bot to balance Thorchain & Binance (BepSwap) AMM prices and contributed to the foundation for the AMM design we know today. The co-founders of Thorchain made me take the lead investor role and design the un-lock of seed investors in deploying the tokens to secure the Thorchain network (Thornodes). These contributions shaped my profound understanding of the Cosmos SDK. My initial $500K investment has astoundingly grown to over $120 million.

I served as a key member of the NibiruChain team, where I contributed to the economic framework and the original concept of the DeFi hub. Identified and invested in early stage high value projects in Cosmos like KAVA, BAND, and AKASH.

Market Making and High-Frequency Trading

I spent 3 years as a High-Frequency Trading quant for Louis Dreyfus, Balyasny Asset Management, and Blue Ridge Capital trading commodities such as metals, livestock, “ags”, “soft”, electricity. Managed a $150M portfolio as the CEO of Credence Capital, where I led blockchain efforts. During this time, I led a team to manage cryptocurrency market-making and was a designated market-maker (top 5 in terms of volume) for USDT perps on BINANCE.

Academic

With a Ph.D. from MIT, focused on the research extending the frontiers of machine learning applications to nonlinear dynamical systems. Using MIT's supercomputing facilities to simulate three-dimensional flags was not only a first in such applications but also established new benchmarks for analytical constraints on stability. The PhD work resulted in 10 peer-reviewed publications, in Turbulence—a subject that caught the attention of organizations like NASA. I have a Master's in Finance from the MIT's Sloan School of Management.
